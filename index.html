Project One

ArcPy Machine Learning Spatial Analysis

This project mapped urban heat across Edinburgh and tested whether council-managed tree data improved random-forest temperature prediction beyond an environmental baseline. Improved model preformance would indicate that tree cover is a relavent predictor of land surface temperature.

Tools and techniques: ArcGIS Pro, ArcPy, Forest-based regression, Spatial data cleaning and management.

Data and methods

Council-managed trees were combined with NDVI and NDMI calculated from Sentinel-2 imagary, Landsat 9 land surface temperature, Copernicus elevation data and Edinburgh greenspace location data. Variables were aggregated to 6,500+ four-hectare hexagons using ArcPy. Forest-based regression models compared an environmental baseline of NDVI, NDMI, elevation and greenspace area with models including tree density, canopy coverage and maturity.

Results

The baseline model explained 72.0% of temperature variation. Adding tree density alone reduced performance, while adding canopy cover and maturity produced the highest validation R-squared of 0.726. Elevation, NDMI and NDVI remained the strongest predictors. Canopy coverage added modest predictive value and was more informative than tree density or maturity.
